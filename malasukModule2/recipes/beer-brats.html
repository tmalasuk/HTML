Beer Brats

Time: 60 minutes

Beer brats are a quintessential Midwestern dish that combines juicy bratwurst sausages with the rich flavors of beer and onions, making them a popular choice for barbecues and gatherings. Typically, the brats are simmered in a mixture of beer—often a lager or pale ale—and sliced onions, allowing them to absorb the savory and slightly bitter notes of the beer while becoming tender and flavorful. After simmering, the sausages are often grilled to achieve a perfect char, enhancing their taste and texture. Served in soft buns and topped with mustard, sauerkraut, or grilled peppers, beer brats exemplify the hearty, comforting nature of Midwestern cuisine and are a staple at summer cookouts and tailgating events.

Ingredients

  • 5 uncooked bratwursts
  • 3 (12-ounce) cans of beer (lager is recommended)
  • 1 medium onion, cut into rings
  • 1/8 cup yellow mustard
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 large red bell pepper, cut into rings (optional)
  • 5 hot dog buns or hoagie rolls
  • Sauerkraut (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beer Mixture: In a large pot or Dutch oven, combine the beer, sliced onions, Worcestershire sauce, and brown sugar. Stir well to combine.
  2. Simmer the Brats: Add the uncooked bratwursts to the pot. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Allow the brats to simmer for about 15-20 minutes, ensuring they are cooked through and infused with the beer flavor.
  3. Grill the Brats: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the brats from the beer mixture and place them on the grill. Grill the brats for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are nicely browned and have grill marks.
  4. Optional Grilled Vegetables: If using, add the red bell pepper rings to the grill during the last few minutes of cooking, allowing them to soften and develop a bit of char.
  5. Assemble the Brats: Spread the yellow and stone ground mustard on the hot dog buns or hoagie rolls. Place a grilled brat in each bun and top with grilled onions and bell peppers, if desired.
  6. Serve: Optionally, serve with sauerkraut on the side or on top of the brats. Enjoy your delicious beer brats with your favorite sides and beverages!
