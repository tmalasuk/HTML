Pot Roast

Time: 3 hours

Pot roast is a quintessential comfort food that embodies the heartiness and warmth of home-cooked meals. This classic dish typically features a large cut of beef, most commonly a chuck roast, which is seasoned and seared before being slow-cooked with a medley of vegetables such as carrots, potatoes, and onions. The slow-cooking process allows the tough fibers of the meat to break down, resulting in a tender, flavorful roast that practically falls apart at the touch of a fork. The addition of beef broth, herbs, and sometimes a splash of red wine enhances the dish's depth of flavor, making it a favorite for family gatherings and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 3 to 4 pounds beef chuck roast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups carrots, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 cups potatoes, cut into chunks (Yukon gold or red potatoes work well)
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 2 bay leaves

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 275°F (135°C).
  2. Sear the Roast: In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Season the beef chuck roast generously with salt and pepper. Once the oil is hot, add the roast and sear it on all sides until browned, about 4-5 minutes per side. Remove the roast from the pot and set it aside.
  3. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in about 1 cup of beef broth, scraping the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. This adds flavor to the dish.
  5. Add Remaining Ingredients: Return the seared roast to the pot. Add the remaining beef broth, carrots, potatoes, celery, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, rosemary, and bay leaves. Stir gently to combine.
  6. Cook the Pot Roast: Cover the pot with a lid and transfer it to the preheated oven. Cook for about 3 to 4 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily pulls apart with a fork.
  7. Serve: Once cooked, remove the pot from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes. Discard the bay leaves before serving. Slice the pot roast and serve it with the vegetables and broth for a hearty meal.
