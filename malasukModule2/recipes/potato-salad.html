Potato Salad

Time: 30 minutes

Potato salad is a beloved side dish that features tender, boiled potatoes mixed with creamy mayonnaise, tangy mustard, and a variety of crunchy vegetables such as celery and red onion, often enhanced with seasonings like salt, pepper, and dill. This versatile dish is a staple at picnics, barbecues, and potlucks, celebrated for its comforting flavors and ability to complement a wide range of main courses

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds Yukon gold or red potatoes
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 11 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper (or to taste)
  • 1/2 cup celery, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill or parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Potatoes: Begin by washing the potatoes thoroughly. Cut them into evenly sized chunks (about 1 to 2 inches) to ensure even cooking. Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold water. Add a pinch of salt and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat and simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender but not mushy.
  2. Drain and Cool: Once cooked, drain the potatoes in a colander and let them cool for about 10 minutes. Allowing them to cool slightly helps prevent the mayonnaise from separating when mixed.
  3. Prepare the Dressing: In a large mixing bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, salt, and black pepper. Mix well until smooth and creamy.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Once the potatoes have cooled, gently fold them into the dressing, ensuring they are well coated. Add the chopped celery, red onion, and hard-boiled eggs (if using) to the mixture, stirring gently to combine without mashing the potatoes.
  5. Chill and Serve: Cover the potato salad and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Before serving, taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Garnish with fresh dill or parsley, if desired.
